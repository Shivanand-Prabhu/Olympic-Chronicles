Paris 2024


Welcome to Olympic Chronicles

The 2024 Summer Olympics, known as Paris 2024, took place from July 26 to August 11 in France. Around 10,714 athletes from 204 nations, including the Athletes’ Independent Neutral (AIN) and Olympic Refugee (EOR) teams, participated. The opening ceremony at Stade de France highlighted French culture. New sports like breakdancing debuted. Athletes competed in 329 events across 33 sports, with many record-breaking performances. France finished in the top five of the medal tally. The closing ceremony celebrated athletic achievements and international unity, emphasizing sustainability and innovation.

  • New Sports: Breaking (breakdancing) made its Olympic debut, adding a fresh and dynamic element to the Games.
  • Sustainability Initiatives: Paris 2024 aimed to be the greenest Olympics ever, focusing on existing venues and renewable energy.
  • Emissions Reduction: Organizers aimed to cut the carbon footprint by 50% compared to previous Games.
  • Venue Utilization: 95% of venues were existing or temporary, minimizing environmental impact.
  • Renewable Energy: All venues were powered by renewable sources, ensuring a low carbon footprint.

Why it is unique?

  • Global Participation: The Olympics bring together athletes from over 200 countries, making it one of the most inclusive and diverse sporting events in the world. This global participation fosters a sense of unity and international camaraderie.
  • Variety of Sports: The Games feature a wide range of sports, from traditional events like and swimming to newer additions like skateboarding and breaking (breakdancing). This variety ensures there is something for everyone to enjoy
  • Cultural Exchange: The Olympics serve as a melting pot of cultures, where athletes and spectators from different bacrounds come together, share their traditions, and learn from each other. This cultural exchange promotes mutual understanding and respect.
  • Historical Significance: The Olympics have a rich history that dates back to ancient Greece, making them a symbol of enduring human achievement and tradition. The modern Games continue this legacy, celebrating both athletic excellence and historical continuity.
  • Promotion of Peace and Unity: The Olympics are more than just a sporting event; they are a platform for promoting peace, solidarity, and fair play on a global scale. The Olympic Village, where athletes live together during the Games, exemplifies this spirit of unity in diversity.

The Paris 2024 Olympic Games will showcase a variety of iconic venues, blending historic landmarks with modern sports facilities. The Stade de France will host athletics and rugby, while the Champs-Élysées and Place de la Concorde will be the site for road cycling and other ouspanoor events. The Eiffel Tower will serve as a backdrop for beach volleyball, enhancing the city's charm. Tennis will take place at the famous Roland Garros Stadium, and swimming will be held at the newly built Aquatics Centre. These venues highlight Paris’s rich cultural heritage and innovative infrastructure for a spectacular global event.
